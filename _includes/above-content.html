About this book

King's Cross from below is an open-access academic book documenting four decades of community action around the redevelopment of King's Cross in London. The book brings together 36 contributors - activists, residents, planners, and scholars - to tell the story of how communities engaged with, resisted, and shaped one of London's most significant urban transformations.

This website serves as a living archive for the book. It is published by Michael Edwards and Jason Katz and will be preserved as part of the British Library's digital archive. Each chapter has a permanent URL for citation purposes. The website provides an accessible, archival-quality format designed for both online reading and printing.
