I still feel squatters should be replaced by shortlife licencees. Firstly this was the fact; there were no squatters in Stanley Buildings, everyone had a rent book. But this also makes it clear that we were there by agreement with Camden Council. There were advantages in this arrangement for the council as negotiating with SCH for vacant possession when the funding for renovation finally came through from central government was easier and cheaper than having to take legal action to evict squatters form every individual property. This was all part of a political climate where it was accepted that local authorities would be constantly buying and upgrading substandard property consequently a supply of empty properties would always be available. 

Leaving squatters in the text plays into the trope of depicting us as anarchic, anti-establishment long haired hippies. We become romanticised or demonised depending on your political point of view. This stops the reader from understanding more fully the social and political context in which these circumstances arose.
