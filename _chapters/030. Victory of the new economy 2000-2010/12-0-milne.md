## Discussion Thread

Robert Milne (30/4/26): (The 'draft response' contains just the views of named individuals from named groups; it does not contain any DF view,. The 'formal response' (submitted in November 2006, apparently) contains slightly more individual views but does not name the individuals or the groups; it does contain the DF view but that is not 'people were broadly in support'. On the contrary, it said 'The Forum welcomed in principle the redevelopment of the Kings Cross Central site but, despite subsequent amendments resulting in the current application, is still not in a position either to welcome or reject the current outline application as a whole. As the responses show there is overwhelming support for some proposals and equally strong objections to others. A number of issues show mixed views. The Forum believes the application still needs further revision and that more information should be provided (and particularly on securing mechanisms for monitoring, community facilities and involvement) before the application can be determined by the planning authorities.' 
In particular, it is not obvious that the DF view carried greater weight, as otherwise the officers could have reported that the DF thought that the application needed further revision.
Both documents are on the 12 Milne webpage; in fact the quotation above is in Chapter 12. 
I don't have the officers' report (and it is not on the relevant council webpage), so I can't comment on what the officers actually wrote; in the transcript of the meeting they said nothing about the responses from the DF or other organisations. The letter from Salman Gill to the members of the sub-committee on 28 February refers to page 56 of the report as giving a brief summary of the DF views and again says that the DF support some proposals and oppose others.
